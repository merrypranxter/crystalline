# 06 — Monoclinic Crystal System

## Overview

The **monoclinic** system has three axes of all different lengths with **one oblique angle**
(β ≠ 90°) while the other two remain right angles (α = γ = 90°).
The unique 2-fold axis (or mirror plane) is conventionally the b-axis.

This is the most species-rich crystal system — containing P2₁/c (No. 14),
the most common space group for all known crystal structures.

---

## Lattice Parameters

| Parameter | Constraint |
|-----------|-----------|
| a | free |
| b | free (unique axis by convention) |
| c | free |
| α | = 90° |
| β | free (> 90° by convention) |
| γ | = 90° |

**Degrees of freedom:** 4 (a, b, c, β)

Convention: β is the obtuse angle (β > 90°). Some older texts use α instead.

---

## Bravais Lattices

| Symbol | Name | Points/Cell |
|--------|------|------------|
| mP | Primitive monoclinic | 1 |
| mC | C-centered monoclinic | 2 |

The C-centering adds lattice points at the centers of the C faces (ab-plane faces).
Some sources write this as mA or mI, but these are equivalent by axis transformation.

## Point Groups

| H-M Symbol | Schönflies | Order | Type | Piezoelectric |
|-----------|-----------|-------|------|--------------|
| 2 | C₂ | 2 | Monoclinic sphenoidal | Yes |
| m | Cs | 2 | Monoclinic domatic | Yes |
| 2/m | C₂h | 4 | Monoclinic prismatic (holohedric) | No |

Space group **P2₁/c (No. 14)** alone contains ~35% of all known organic crystal
structures and a large fraction of inorganic ones.

---

## Space Groups (3–15)

**3–5: Point group 2**
P2 (3), P2₁ (4), C2 (5)

**6–9: Point group m**
Pm (6), Pc (7), Cm (8), Cc (9)

**10–15: Point group 2/m**
P2/m (10), P2₁/m (11), C2/m (12), P2/c (13), **P2₁/c (14)** — most common,
**C2/c (15)** — pyroxenes, amphiboles, micas

## Pyroxene vs Amphibole Comparison

Both groups are monoclinic C2/c or C2/m silicates, related structurally:

| Feature | Pyroxenes | Amphiboles |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Chain type | Single SiO₃ | Double Si₄O₁₁ |
| OH groups | None | 2 per formula |
| β angle | ~105–107° | ~104–106° |
| Cleavage | Two at ~87° & ~93° | Two at ~56° & ~124° |
| Cleavage angle | Near 90° | Near 60° and 120° |
| Example | Diopside, Augite, Jadeite | Hornblende, Actinolite, Tremolite |

The cleavage angles (87/93° for pyroxene vs 56/124° for amphibole) allow identification
in thin section under the microscope.

## Mathematical Formulas

### d-Spacing (monoclinic)
```
1/d²_hkl = (1/sin²β)·[h²/a² + k²sin²β/b² + l²/c² - 2hl·cosβ/(ac)]
```

### Unit Cell Volume
```
V = a · b · c · sin(β)
```

### Reciprocal lattice parameters
```
a* = 1/(a·sin β)
b* = 1/b
c* = 1/(c·sin β)
β* = 180° − β
```

## Visual Characteristics

- **Crystal habit:** Prismatic, tabular, blade-like, platy
- **Cleavage:** Often two good cleavages — diagnostic for pyroxenes/amphiboles
- **Optical:** Biaxial — 2V angle and extinction angle diagnostic
- **Gypsum:** Satin spar (fibrous), alabaster (fine-grained), selenite (clear plates)
- **Micas:** Perfect {001} basal cleavage, elastic flexible sheets
- **Common twinning:** Swallow-tail twin in gypsum; Carlsbad twin in orthoclase
